# Pathogen Dominion - Scientific Mapping Notes

## Core scientific principles used

1. **Tissue tropism and compatibility**
   - Regions have environmental constraints (pH, oxygen, barrier level, immune surveillance, mucus).
   - Colonization chance depends on pathogen traits + adaptations + route access.

2. **Anatomical spread pathways**
   - Movement is constrained by adjacency and route types (`mucosal`, `blood`, `lymph`, `neural`, `surface`).
   - Certain regions (e.g., CNS) require specific unlocks such as `neural_tropism`.

3. **Immune system as dynamic opponent**
   - Local clearance combines tissue immune pressure, visibility, and global attention.
   - Adaptive pressure increases over time and repeated visibility.
   - Treatment events apply abstract strategic pressure without procedural medical detail.

4. **Tradeoff-based adaptation**
   - Adaptation nodes provide benefits (e.g., bloodstream survival, dormancy, resistance) while applying costs (visibility, upkeep, reduced replication, reduced stealth, etc.).

5. **Host viability constraint**
   - Symptoms and inflammation lower host viability each turn.
   - Over-aggressive virulence can cause early host death, reducing strategic outcomes.

## Gameplay abstractions / fictionalization

- Full-host “domination victory” is fictionalized for grand-strategy framing.
- Resource terms (Biomass, Diversity, Stealth, Immune Attention) are high-level strategy abstractions, not clinical quantities.
- Immune and treatment mechanics are represented as game-pressure systems rather than medical protocols.
- Region geometry is stylized node-atlas design for clarity, not anatomical simulation fidelity.

## Safety and content boundaries

- No actionable laboratory instructions are included.
- No procedural treatment dosing or protocol modeling is included.
- Terminology is descriptive and systems-level to remain educational and non-operational.
